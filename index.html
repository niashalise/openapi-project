Foodie or Fruity?

Click the fruit name for health information. Click the button at the bottom of the page for other food.

Mango

Fun fact: Mango trees can grow up to 100 feet tall and live for over 300 years while still bearing fruit.

Banana

Fun fact: Despite their size and shape, botanically, bananas are classified as berries!

Apple

Fun fact: Apples are grown on every continent except Antarctica, making them one of the most widely cultivated fruits in the world.

Pineapple

Fun fact: Botanically, pineapples are considered a type of berry, formed by the fusion of many individual flowers on the plant.

Orange

Fun fact: Oranges are not a naturally occurring fruit but a hybrid between pomelo and mandarin.

Kiwi

Fun fact: The kiwifruit originated in China, where it was known as "Chinese gooseberry." It wasn’t until the 20th century that it became widely cultivated in New Zealand. The fruit was renamed "kiwi" after New Zealand’s national bird, the kiwi, because they both have brown, fuzzy skin.

Strawberry

Fun fact: Strawberries are made up of about 90% water, making them a hydrating and low-calorie snack.

Grape

Fun fact: Grapes have been used to make wine for over 6,000 years. The first known vineyard was in what is now Georgia (the country).

Lychee

Fun fact: Just a small serving of lychee provides more than 100% of the daily recommended intake of vitamin C, making it a great immunity booster.

Watermelon

Fun fact: The largest watermelon ever recorded weighed 350.5 pounds (159 kg) and was grown by Chris Kent of Tennessee in 2013.

Papaya

Fun fact: Papayas are packed with nutrients. They are high in vitamin C, vitamin A, folate, and fiber, and contain an enzyme called papain that aids digestion.

Lime

Fun fact: Lime juice has natural antibacterial properties and can be used as a natural cleaner or disinfectant in addition to its culinary uses.
